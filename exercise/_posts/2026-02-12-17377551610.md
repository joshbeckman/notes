When I woke up this morning, my heel hurt a bites than yesterday, but it still hurt. I'm still wearing the Straussburg Sock every night, which I hate. But maybe it's helping.

In this physical therapy session, Ruth did a running analysis of my comfortable jogging pace. I ran on the treadmill for about 5min and then she took some slow-motion video of my running and footfalls and then analyzed the video while I did other recovery exercises.

Ruth found that I have a cadence of about 152 steps per minute, and I should aim for 170 or slightly above that. The optimal running cadence averages between 170 and 180 steps per minute for people of average height ([see more](https://www.nike.com/a/what-is-cadence-running#:~:text=the%20optimal%20running%20cadence%20averages%20between%20170%20and%20180%20steps%20per%20minute%20for%20people%20of%20average%20height%2C%20which%20in%20the%20United%20States%20is%205%20feet%204%20inches%20for%20women%20and%20about%205%20feet%209%20inches%20for%20men.%20Running%20within%20this%20range%20may%20reduce%20injury%20risk%20by%20shortening%20ground%20contact%20time.)). So, I'm going to work on slowly increasing that rate to reduce ground contact time. I should only increase it by 10% at a time, over the course of weeks, and I can do that by choosing a song at a target BPM and then running to that.


I looked back on my months of running data and apparently I’ve been consistently around 155spm for the last year. Interesting. I never knew about this average/target. 
 She also says that I'm moving my arms more than I need to, and that it's a waste of energy, so I can cut down on that. Otherwise, running analysis looks good! I need to strengthen the inner lower-leg muscles so that my foot doesn't slap down from the outer edge to the inner edge as quickly.  It's a normal motion but mine might be rolling a bit quickly.
