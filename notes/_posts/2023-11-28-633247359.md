TTLs have traditionally been implemented using relative time (*in 10 seconds*, or in steps as with [IP](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc791)) rather than absolute time (*until 09:54:10 UTC*) because comparing absolute times across machines is risky. The downside of the relative approach is that everybody needs to measure the time taken and remember to decrease the TTL, which adds complexity. High quality clocks fix the drift problem, and allow us to use absolute time TTLs.
