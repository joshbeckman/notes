After buying it on release and for my graduation, I had and loved my [Fuji X100](https://kenrockwell.com/fuji/x100.htm) for 10 years. It was the perfect camera for me for many years.

Then, in 2021, I was entering a new chapter of my life and the X100 was slowing me down. I tried out ([renting through Lensrentals](https://share.lensrentals.com/x/8s09Zi)) its newer sibling but was annoyed with the cluttered interface. I also rented a [Leica Q](https://craigmod.com/essays/leica_q/) and [Q2](https://leica-camera.com/en-US/photography/cameras/q/q2-black). At the time, the Q was already outdated but I was annoyed with the button layout of its contemporary, the Q2. I found a high-quality used [Q-P version](https://www.dpreview.com/products/leica/compacts/leica_q-p) and bit the high-price bullet.

I have loved the Q very much and taken it a great many places. It's been to the [top of the rockies](https://www.joshbeckman.org/blog/traveling/cirque-of-the-towers-2024) and to [the bottom of the Grand Canyon](https://www.joshbeckman.org/blog/traveling/hiking-the-grand-canyon-royal-arch-loop) and multiple continents. But it wasn't state-of-the-art when I first bought it and it's only gotten longer in the tooth. I love the size of the stills, but the video output is very weak (small size, bad focus, poor audio) and the in-camera JPEG creation is very bad. I have always shot RAW-only with it and then output JPEGs in my laptop. I don't even do coloring or filters - it's just that the camera's JPEGs were always so under-saturated and had no settings to tweak that output.

With the arrival of our first kid, I immediately wanted to take more and higher-quality video and to more easily access+share the photos I get from my camera. The old Q is bad at both those things, so I started looking for a replacement.

I [talked about it with Perplexity off and on](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/i-have-a-leica-q-and-want-to-u-Nx1_gs8USOah0EWKjVRzYw#0) for a couple days. I considered the [Sony RX1RIII](https://electronics.sony.com/imaging/compact-cameras/all-vlog-compact-cameras/p/dscrx1rm3b) (incredibly compact but lacking the tactile controls I love so very much), the [Fujifilm GFX100RF](https://www.fujifilm-x.com/en-us/products/cameras/gfx100rf/) (really tempting to move up to a medium-format sensor ([Noah Kalina](https://www.joshbeckman.org/search/?q=%22Noah+kalina%22&keys=&type=&category=&after=&before=)'s work has been inspiring me), but I think I would miss the low-light performance and I would get annoyed dealing with such huge files), the [Fujifilm X-E5](https://www.fujifilm-x.com/en-us/products/cameras/x-e5/) (the lack of manual controls and need to manage interchangeable lenses would annoy me), and of course the [Fujifilm X100VI](https://www.fujifilm-x.com/en-us/products/cameras/x100vi/). The X100VI is the obvious choice to return to that original compact digital rangefinder, but I had [rented it back in October](https://www.joshbeckman.org/blog/traveling/pittsburgh-fall-2025) and A) I didn't find it _that_ much more compact than the Q body and B) I got annoyed with the fly-by-wire manual focusing and C) I surprisingly no longer loved the optical viewfinder.

Leica released [the Q3](https://leica-camera.com/en-US/photography/cameras/q/q3-black) in the years since I bought the Q, and updated most aspects of the Q2. So, I rented the Leica Q3 [through Lensrentals](https://share.lensrentals.com/x/8s09Zi) for a week test its new controls and output.

![Me holding the rented Leica Q3 for a test shot](/assets/images/4ab0d9fe-a469-4e6f-b09a-4fda72476f57.jpeg)

After a day, I knew the Q3 was the upgrade I would love. They solved all my control-layout problems from the Q2 in this Q3 body. The ISO control under-thumb is perfect and the video output is lovely ([Van Neistat](https://www.youtube.com/@vanneistat) has been using it quite a lot in recent videos). The film simulations and in-camera JPEG output tweaks _should_ mean I can use JPEGs straight-outta-camera again!

I got an initial quote from Adorama on a trade-in of my Q for the Q3. I packaged up the old Q and sent it on its way, swathed in pink wrapping paper. A couple days later, a sales rep from Adorama called me and told me I had underestimated the wear on my Q (*sigh*, not surprising) but that he could get me a discount on an opened-box Q3. I bit the bullet and the Q3 should arrive next week.

I'm looking forward to faster-downloads, bigger prints, and beautiful videos.
